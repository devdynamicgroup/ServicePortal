About This Standard

The values shown are based on the DWQI (Drinking Water Quality Index) — an international framework derived from WHO guidelines that evaluates your water across multiple parameters at once.

How it differs from Thai standards:

Thailand's official standard (กรมอนามัย / MWA) allows higher limits on some parameters — for example, free chlorine up to 1.5 mg/L vs. WHO's ≤ 0.5 mg/L. Your tap water may meet Thai regulations but still score low on the DWQI.

We use the DWQI because it reflects what's considered safe and comfortable for long-term daily use.
